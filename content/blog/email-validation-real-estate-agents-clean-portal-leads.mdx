# Email Validation for Real Estate Agents: How to Clean Portal Leads and Reduce Bounced Emails

Real estate is one of the few industries where speed to lead determines whether you close a deal or lose it to the agent down the street. When a prospect submits an inquiry on Zillow, Realtor.com, or through a Facebook lead ad, the clock starts immediately. Studies consistently show that responding within five minutes dramatically increases conversion rates.

The problem is that a significant percentage of leads coming through these portals contain email addresses that will never receive your follow-up. Fake addresses, disposable emails, old accounts, and typos fill your CRM with contacts who are impossible to reach. If you are loading those addresses directly into your drip campaigns and nurture sequences, you are damaging your sender reputation with every batch you send.

## Why Real Estate Portal Leads Have Worse Email Quality Than You Think

Most real estate agents assume that if someone filled out a form on Zillow requesting information about a property, they provided a real email address. That assumption costs teams thousands of dollars in wasted outreach and degraded deliverability every year.

**Casual browsing generates junk submissions.** Buyers browse listings the same way people scroll social media. They tap "request info" on a property they find interesting without serious intent. When the form asks for an email, they type the first thing that comes to mind, often a throwaway address they use specifically for situations where they expect marketing emails.

**Seller valuation tools attract curiosity, not commitment.** Home valuation widgets on Realtor.com and similar portals are designed to capture seller leads. Homeowners enter their address to see an estimate, then provide an email to unlock the result. Many of these users are just curious about their home value and have no intention of selling. They use whatever email address involves the least future inconvenience, which frequently means a disposable or rarely checked inbox.

**Phone number quality does not predict email quality.** Some agents assume that if the phone number connects, the email must be valid too. These are independent data points. A prospect might provide their real phone number because they expect a call, but enter a throwaway email because they do not want automated drip campaigns filling their inbox.

**Corporate relocation leads often use expiring work addresses.** When companies relocate employees, the leads generated through corporate relocation portals frequently include work email addresses. If the employee is changing companies as part of the move, that work email may expire before your nurture sequence even finishes running.

## The Real Cost for Real Estate Teams

Email deliverability problems compound faster in real estate than in most industries because of how agents typically use email.

**Drip campaigns run for months.** A typical real estate nurture sequence runs 6 to 18 months. Every invalid address in that sequence generates repeated bounces over time, each one further damaging your sender domain. By the time you notice the problem, your domain reputation has been eroding for weeks or months.

**IDX and CRM integrations auto-enroll leads.** Most real estate CRMs (Follow Up Boss, Sierra Interactive, kvCORE, BoomTown) automatically enroll new leads into email sequences. There is no manual review step between lead capture and first email. Invalid addresses go straight into active campaigns.

**Team accounts amplify the damage.** On a team of five agents sharing a brokerage domain for outreach, one agent importing a dirty Zillow lead list can tank deliverability for the entire team. When the shared domain gets flagged, everyone's emails start landing in spam folders, including transaction-related communications with active clients.

**Transaction emails get caught in the crossfire.** Real estate agents use the same email infrastructure for marketing and for critical transaction communications: contract updates, inspection reports, closing documents. If your domain reputation drops because of bad marketing lists, your transaction emails suffer too.

## The Validation Workflow for Real Estate Leads

Here is the process that protects your email infrastructure while ensuring you reach every legitimate prospect as fast as possible.

### Step 1: Validate at the Point of Entry

The best time to validate an email address is before it enters your CRM. If your lead capture system supports webhook integrations or Zapier connections, insert a validation step between the form submission and the CRM record creation.

For Zillow Premier Agent leads, Realtor.com leads, and similar portal integrations, you typically cannot validate before the lead enters your CRM because the integration pushes directly. In these cases, set up an automated validation trigger that runs within minutes of lead creation.

### Step 2: Run Standard Verification First

Pass every new lead email through standard SMTP verification. This immediately identifies addresses with invalid syntax, non-existent domains, and mailboxes that definitively do not exist. For most real estate lead sources, standard verification will flag 10 to 20 percent of addresses as invalid.

Remove these addresses from your active drip campaigns immediately. Do not attempt to send to them.

### Step 3: Handle Catch-All and Unknown Results

After standard verification, you will have a segment of addresses marked as catch-all or unknown. This is particularly common with leads from corporate domains (relocation leads, investor inquiries, commercial real estate prospects) where the mail server accepts all incoming email regardless of whether the specific mailbox exists.

Standard verification tools cannot tell you whether these addresses are real. [Scrubby](https://scrubby.io) resolves this gap by analyzing catch-all addresses through behavioral deliverability testing. For a typical real estate lead list, Scrubby identifies which catch-all addresses actually receive mail and which are phantom inboxes that will silently fail.

This step is critical for commercial real estate teams and agents working corporate relocation accounts, where 30 to 50 percent of email addresses may be on catch-all domains.

### Step 4: Segment and Route by Quality

After validation, organize your leads into action tiers:

- **Verified valid**: Enroll immediately in your standard drip campaign. These are confirmed deliverable addresses that should receive your fastest follow-up.
- **Catch-all verified deliverable**: Enroll in your drip campaign with monitoring. These addresses passed Scrubby's deliverability check but warrant a 24-hour watch on bounce behavior.
- **Invalid or undeliverable**: Do not email these contacts. If the lead includes a phone number, switch to phone-only follow-up. If no phone number is available, suppress the record entirely.

### Step 5: Re-validate Before Re-engagement Campaigns

Real estate agents regularly run re-engagement campaigns against older leads: "Still looking?" sequences, market update emails, anniversary check-ins. Before sending to any list that has not been validated in the past 90 days, run it through verification again. Email address decay is particularly aggressive in real estate because prospects change email providers, abandon addresses after purchasing, or switch from personal to professional email (or vice versa) during major life transitions.

## Integrating Validation with Your Existing Stack

The goal is to make validation invisible to your daily workflow while protecting every email you send.

**For agents using Follow Up Boss, Sierra, or kvCORE**: Set up a Zapier workflow that triggers on new lead creation, sends the email through validation, and tags the lead with the result. Configure your drip campaigns to exclude leads tagged as invalid.

**For teams running cold outreach through tools like [Kali](https://getkali.io)**: Validate your prospecting lists before importing them into your outreach tool. Calendar invite outreach is a powerful channel for reaching real estate decision-makers (investors, developers, property managers), but only if the underlying contact data is clean.

**For brokerages managing marketing through [Vendisys](https://vendisys.com)**: Feed validated leads into your outbound pipeline rather than managing email sending independently. Centralized pipeline management prevents the fragmented sending infrastructure that causes deliverability problems for multi-agent teams.

**For teams tracking competitive listings with [CAM](https://getcam.io)**: When you identify competitor activity and want to reach out to prospects in affected markets, make sure those prospect lists are validated before outreach. Competitive intelligence is only valuable if you can actually reach the people you are targeting.

## Common Mistakes Real Estate Teams Make

**Trusting portal "verified" labels.** Some lead providers claim their leads are "verified." This typically means the email passed a basic syntax check or that the lead submitted a confirmation step. It does not mean the email address is currently deliverable.

**Validating once and never again.** Email validity is not permanent. An address that was valid six months ago may not be valid today. Build re-validation into your quarterly CRM hygiene process.

**Ignoring the problem because phone works.** Many real estate agents rely primarily on phone calls and text messages. They tolerate bad email data because "I just call them anyway." The issue is that your email reputation still degrades with every bounced drip email, and when you need email to work (transaction updates, market reports, referral requests), it may already be compromised.

**Bulk importing purchased lists without validation.** Agents who buy "homeowner lists" or "expired listing leads" from data providers are importing the riskiest email data available. These lists have the highest rates of invalid addresses, spam traps, and addresses that will generate complaints. Never send to a purchased list without full validation first.

## Protect Your Pipeline and Your Reputation

Real estate is a relationship business built on communication. When your emails bounce or land in spam, you are not just losing that one lead. You are undermining the infrastructure you use to communicate with every prospect, client, and referral partner in your database.

Validate every lead before it enters your active campaigns. Handle catch-all addresses properly with [Scrubby](https://scrubby.io). Segment by quality. Re-validate before re-engagement. The leads from your portals are valuable, but only the ones with real email addresses attached to them.
