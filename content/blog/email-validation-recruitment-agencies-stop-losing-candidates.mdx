# Email Validation for Recruitment Agencies: Stop Losing Candidates to Bounced Emails

A recruiter finds the perfect candidate for a senior engineering role. The client has been waiting three weeks. The recruiter sends an outreach email to the candidate's work address, follows up twice, and hears nothing. Two weeks later, the candidate accepts a competing offer. When the recruiter finally checks, the email address was invalid the entire time. The messages never arrived.

This scenario plays out constantly in recruitment. Unlike most industries where a bounced email means a missed marketing impression, in staffing and recruiting a bounced email can mean a lost placement worth $15,000 to $40,000 in fees. The stakes per contact are extraordinarily high, and most agencies do almost nothing to validate their email data before sending.

## Why Recruitment Email Data Is Uniquely Unreliable

Recruitment agencies face email data quality problems that other industries do not encounter at the same scale.

**Job changers invalidate emails faster than any other segment.** The candidates recruiters contact are, by definition, people who change jobs. Every time a candidate moves to a new company, their old work email stops working. In a typical recruitment database, 20 to 30 percent of candidate work emails go stale within 12 months. Compare that to a B2B marketing list where the average decay rate is closer to 2 to 3 percent per month. Recruiters are working with the fastest-degrading contact data in any industry.

**ATS systems accumulate dead addresses over years.** Most agencies run applicant tracking systems that have been collecting candidate records for five, ten, or even fifteen years. These databases contain hundreds of thousands of email addresses, and nobody is systematically cleaning them. When a recruiter pulls a candidate from the ATS for a new search, they are often working with an email address that was valid when the candidate applied to a different role three years ago.

**Client-side contacts change frequently too.** Hiring managers move between companies, get promoted out of their hiring role, or leave organizations entirely. The client contact list that was accurate six months ago may have 15 to 20 percent invalid addresses today. Sending placement confirmations, interview schedules, or contract details to dead email addresses delays the entire hiring process.

**Scraped and enriched data has high error rates.** Many recruiters supplement their ATS data with emails gathered from LinkedIn enrichment tools, job board exports, and web scraping services. These sources have variable accuracy. Some enrichment providers return "best guess" email addresses constructed from name patterns (firstname.lastname@company.com) without verifying whether the specific mailbox exists. The result is a contact list where 10 to 25 percent of addresses have never been valid at all.

## The Real Cost of Bounced Emails in Recruitment

The financial impact goes beyond the obvious missed placement.

**Sender reputation degrades with every bounce.** When your agency's email domain sends messages that bounce, mailbox providers (Gmail, Outlook, corporate mail servers) take notice. A bounce rate above 2 percent starts degrading your sender reputation. Above 5 percent, your emails begin landing in spam folders even for recipients with valid addresses. For a recruitment agency that relies on email for candidate sourcing, client communication, and interview coordination, degraded deliverability affects every aspect of the business.

**Candidates who never receive your message accept other offers.** In a competitive talent market, speed determines placement success. If your initial outreach bounces and you do not discover the failure for days, the candidate has already engaged with other recruiters. You are not just losing one email; you are losing the entire candidate relationship before it begins.

**Client trust erodes when communication fails.** Sending interview confirmations, offer details, or placement updates to invalid client addresses makes your agency look disorganized. Clients expect seamless communication, and "the email must have bounced" is not an explanation that inspires confidence in a $25,000 placement fee.

**Compliance exposure increases.** Recruitment agencies operating under GDPR, CAN-SPAM, or similar regulations need to demonstrate that they are maintaining clean, accurate contact data. Repeatedly sending emails to addresses that bounce signals poor data hygiene practices, which can become a liability during audits. ## The Catch-All Problem in Corporate Email

Here is where recruitment email validation gets particularly tricky. A large percentage of enterprise companies, exactly the organizations where recruiters are trying to reach hiring managers and candidates, use catch-all email configurations.

A catch-all domain accepts mail sent to any address at that domain, whether or not the specific mailbox exists. So if you send an email to `nonexistent.person@bigcorp.com`, the domain's mail server will accept the message rather than rejecting it. Standard email verification tools query the mail server and get an "accepted" response, so they report the address as valid. But the email may land in a dead-end mailbox, get silently discarded, or trigger an internal bounce that you never see.

For recruiters, this is a nightmare. You think your outreach was delivered because your verification tool said the address was valid, but the candidate never received anything. You follow up, wait, and eventually move on, never knowing the issue was deliverability rather than interest.

Standard verification tools mark these addresses as "catch-all" or "accept-all" and leave the decision to you. Most recruiters either send to all of them (risking bounces and reputation damage) or suppress all of them (losing access to candidates at major enterprises).

## Building a Validation Workflow for Your Agency

Here is the practical process every recruitment agency should implement.

### Before Every Outreach Campaign

**Step 1: Export and deduplicate.** Pull your candidate or client list from your ATS, CRM, or enrichment tool. Remove exact duplicates on the email field. Strip formatting issues (extra spaces, uppercase inconsistencies).

**Step 2: Run standard email verification.** Feed the list through a verification service that checks syntax, domain validity, MX records, and SMTP responses. Remove all addresses flagged as invalid, disposable, or role-based (like info@ or careers@). This pass typically removes 10 to 20 percent of a recruitment list.

**Step 3: Process catch-all addresses separately.** Take every address flagged as "catch-all" or "accept-all" and run it through Scrubby's dedicated catch-all validation. This step is critical for recruitment because so many enterprise domains use catch-all configurations. You will recover a significant portion of valid candidate addresses that would otherwise be lost or risky to send to.

**Step 4: Segment and send.** Split your validated list into verified addresses and Scrubby-confirmed deliverable addresses. Send to both groups, but monitor the Scrubby-confirmed segment separately for the first campaign to build confidence in the results.

### Ongoing Maintenance

**Re-validate your ATS database quarterly.** Set a calendar reminder to export your active candidate pipeline and run it through validation every 90 days. Given how quickly recruitment contacts go stale, quarterly cleaning prevents your database from accumulating dead addresses that will damage deliverability when you eventually reach out.

**Validate before re-engaging dormant candidates.** If you are reaching back out to candidates you have not contacted in more than 60 days, re-validate their email addresses first. A candidate who was active in your pipeline six months ago may have changed jobs, and their old email is now invalid.

**Monitor bounce rates per campaign.** Track bounce rates for every outreach sequence. If any campaign exceeds 2 percent bounces, pause it immediately and re-validate the remaining unsent addresses. **Clean client contact lists semi-annually.** Your hiring manager and client HR contacts change roles and companies too. Run your client database through validation at least twice a year to ensure placement-related communications (contracts, invoices, interview details) reach the right people.

Not all email validation services handle recruitment data well. Here is what matters for staffing agencies specifically.

**Catch-all resolution is non-negotiable.** If your provider marks catch-all addresses as "unknown" or "risky" and leaves you to guess, you will either suppress too many valid candidates or send to too many invalid ones. You need a provider that can give you a definitive deliverability verdict for catch-all addresses. This is exactly what Scrubby specializes in.

**Bulk processing speed matters.** Recruitment campaigns are time-sensitive. If your validation provider takes 48 hours to process a 10,000-address list, you have lost two days of competitive advantage. Look for providers that deliver results in hours, not days.

**API access enables automation.** The most efficient agencies integrate validation directly into their ATS or CRM workflow so that every new candidate email is validated on entry rather than in batch. API access lets you build this into your existing tools without manual exports and imports.

**Accuracy at enterprise domains is essential.** Recruitment lists are disproportionately weighted toward corporate email addresses at mid-size and enterprise companies. Your validation provider needs strong accuracy on these domains, not just on consumer email (Gmail, Yahoo) where verification is straightforward.
