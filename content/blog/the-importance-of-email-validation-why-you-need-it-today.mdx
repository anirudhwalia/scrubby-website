## **Why is Email Validation Important?**

Email validation is the process of checking if an email address is valid and deliverable. It involves verifying that the email format is correct, the domain exists, and the recipient mailbox can receive emails. **This process is crucial for maintaining a clean and healthy email list**, as it helps reduce bounce rates, minimize spam complaints, and improve overall deliverability. By ensuring that your emails reach the intended recipients, you can increase the effectiveness of your email campaigns and enhance your brand reputation. 

Email validation is a proactive step that every business should take to optimize their email marketing efforts and achieve greater success in reaching their target audience.

### **How is Email Validation done?**

Typically, software tools like [**Scrubby, ZeroBounce, Neverbounce**](https://scrubby.io/blog/neverbounce-vs-zerobounce-vs-scrubby-comparison-review-2024/) are employed to scan through long lists of email addresses and remove invalid ones.

The specific procedure varies from tool to tool, but it generally follows a similar outline:

First, the tool will check for known spam traps, which are email addresses set up to catch senders violating email spamming rules. Generally, this won’t be much of an issue if you’ve acquired your email list organically. But, if you have addresses from different sources in your email database, you should double-check to avoid being blacklisted.

Next, your email validation service will check for invalid email addresses and syntax, like misspellings or typos. After that, the validator will verify the domain name. To do this, it will look at the mail server’s DNS and MX records to verify that the domain exists and can receive emails.

For example, if you have an email with a non-existent domain, the validation tool will check the domain registration. It will proceed with email list cleaning if it doesn’t exist, removing this email from your list.

Finally, the email address validation tool will check individual mailboxes using the SMTP protocol. The tool will ping the mailbox, and if it gets the proper response, it will know that the mailbox exists and is deliverable. If not, it will remove that address.

Email validation is closely related to email address verification, which goes an extra step to check whether the address belongs to someone and is active. Some email verification tools will also check whether an address frequently marks emails as spam. If so, it will flag the address for removal to reduce the risk of spam reporting.

By regularly validating your email list, you can avoid sending emails to invalid addresses or inactive accounts, which can result in high bounce rates and damage your sender reputation. Protect your sender reputation by implementing email validation as a standard practice in your email marketing strategy.

High email deliverability rates are essential for the success of your email campaigns. By regularly validating your email list, you can reduce the chances of your emails being filtered out or blocked by ISPs, ultimately increasing the likelihood of your messages being seen and engaged with. -   **Find Catch-All Email Accounts**

At first, this might not seem like a problem – after all, the email is still delivered. Unfortunately, it’s not that simple. Catch-all email accounts can be configured only to accept emails to invalid addresses temporarily. Then, the user can elect to bounce them back. So good email validators can detect catch-all email accounts and help you avoid the risks associated with them. 

[Catch-all email](https://scrubby.io/blog/understanding-catch-all-emails-what-you-need-to-know/) accounts are email addresses set up to receive all emails sent to a domain – even invalid email addresses. For example, imagine a website with the domain email.com. 

It has only one valid email address: valid@email.com. However, since it is a catch-all account, it will receive mail sent to any address that ends in email.com, even invalid@email.com, notreal@email.com, etc.
