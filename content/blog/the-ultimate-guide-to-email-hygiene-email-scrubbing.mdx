Email hygiene is the practice of regularly cleaning and maintaining your email list to ensure that it contains only valid, engaged recipients. Just as you would clean your home or office to create a pleasant environment, keeping your email list clean helps create a better environment for your email marketing campaigns. Good email hygiene prevents issues such as high bounce rates, spam complaints, and low engagement, all of which can harm your sender reputation and email deliverability.

## **What is Email Hygiene?**

Email hygiene encompasses a range of practices designed to keep your email list healthy and up-to-date. These practices include:

1.  **Removing Invalid Addresses:** Identifying and removing email addresses that are no longer valid or active.
2.  **Eliminating Spam Traps:** Detecting and eliminating email addresses that are designed to identify and catch spammers.
3.  **Segmenting Your List:** Organizing your email list into segments based on engagement levels, demographics, and other criteria to tailor your email campaigns more effectively.
4.  **Monitoring Engagement:** Regularly tracking how recipients interact with your emails and removing those who are consistently unengaged.

### **Why Email List Cleaning Matters**

Regularly cleaning your email list is essential for several reasons:

1.  **Improved Deliverability:** A clean email list reduces bounce rates and spam complaints, which can positively impact your sender reputation and increase the likelihood that your emails will land in the inbox.
2.  **Enhanced Engagement:** By focusing on engaged recipients, you increase the chances of higher open and click-through rates, leading to more successful email campaigns.
3.  **Cost Efficiency:** Many email marketing platforms charge based on the number of subscribers. By removing inactive or invalid addresses, you can reduce costs and improve the return on investment (ROI) of your email marketing efforts.
4.  **Better Data Accuracy:** Keeping your email list clean ensures that your data is accurate and reliable, allowing for more precise targeting and personalization in your campaigns.

## **How to Scrub Your Email List**

Scrubbing your email list involves several steps to ensure that it is clean and healthy. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

1.  **Collect Accurate Data:** Start by ensuring that the data you collect from subscribers is accurate. Use double opt-in methods to verify email addresses at the point of subscription.
2.  **Segment Your List:** Organize your list into segments based on engagement levels, demographics, and other criteria. This allows you to tailor your email campaigns more effectively and identify unengaged recipients.
3.  **Identify Inactive Subscribers:** Determine which subscribers have not engaged with your emails over a specific period. Common indicators of inactivity include not opening emails, not clicking on links, and not responding to calls to action.
4.  **Validate Email Addresses:** Use an email validation tool to check the validity of email addresses. 
5.  **Remove Invalid Addresses:** Once you have identified invalid or risky email addresses with the help of [**Scrubby**](https://scrubby.io/), remove them from your list to prevent bounces and spam complaints.
6.  **Re-engage Inactive Subscribers:** Before removing inactive subscribers, consider sending a re-engagement campaign to win them back. If they remain unengaged, it’s time to remove them from your list.
